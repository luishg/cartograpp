Mapping the
software universe.

The Atlas Constellation is a higher-level layer that organizes, connects, and brings trust to every piece of software worth knowing.

Built on Uptodown’s twenty-plus years of cataloging the open web — and the products being charted on top of it.

§01 · The problem

Software is abundant.
Understanding it is not.

The universe keeps expanding. The chart hasn’t kept up.

Millions of artifacts cross every platform, language, and runtime. There is no shared sky — every store, registry, and silo holds its own private slice of what software is, where it came from, and whether it can be trusted.

Discovery has stopped being the hard problem. The hard questions now are what does this do, can it be trusted, and how does it behave when it runs — and the answers are buried, fragmented, or missing entirely.

As more software is consumed and orchestrated by automated systems and agents, a structured, machine-readable understanding of software becomes infrastructural — not optional.

Volume
Tens of millions of artifacts across every platform, language, and runtime.
Fragmentation
Stores, registries, and silos that don’t speak to each other.
Opacity
Provenance, behavior, and trust are buried — or missing entirely.

§05 · Principles

The commitments
behind the chart.

A neutral observatory only works if it stays that way.

Neutrality

No platform, no kingmakers. Every star charted on the same terms, independent of who ships it.

Trust

Provenance, signatures, and human signal — verifiable, never implied. Reasoning behind every claim, made available.

Accessibility

Legible to people, machines, and the agents in between — across regions, platforms, and use cases.

Intelligence

Not a list of files. A living understanding of the universe — structure that other systems can act on.

§06 · For

For those building
what comes next.

The chart is open to anyone who needs ground truth about software.

Builders

Engineers and platforms wiring software into new products — with one canonical chart underneath instead of a dozen fragmented sources.

Agents

Autonomous systems that need verifiable answers, not scraped guesses. Software intelligence as a first-class input to reasoning.

Enterprises

Teams that require provenance, compliance, and continuity across operating systems, clouds, and vendors — with a chart they can actually audit.

Sovereigns

Governments and institutions charting their own software sky: auditable, deployable intelligence for regulated environments and national programs.
